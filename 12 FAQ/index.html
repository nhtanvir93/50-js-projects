Frequently Asked Questions

What is System Design, and why is it important?

System design is the process of defining the architecture, components, modules, interfaces, and data for a system to satisfy specified requirements. It is critical for building scalable, efficient, and maintainable systems that meet business needs and handle expected (and unexpected) load and complexity.

What are key components of a scalable system design?

  • Load balancers for distributing traffic
  • Caching layers (e.g., Redis, Memcached)
  • Database sharding or replication
  • Asynchronous processing using queues
  • CDNs for static content
  • Microservices architecture for modularity and scalability

How do you design for high availability and fault tolerance?

  • Deploy in multiple availability zones or regions
  • Use redundant systems and failover mechanisms
  • Implement health checks and auto-scaling
  • Design with graceful degradation and retry logic

How do you handle data consistency in distributed systems?

  • CAP theorem trade-offs (Consistency, Availability, Partition tolerance)
  • Eventual consistency models
  • Distributed transactions (e.g., 2PC, Saga pattern)
  • Idempotent operations to handle retries safely
